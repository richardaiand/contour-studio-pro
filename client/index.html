How to Use Contour Studio

1. Dashboard

After signing in, you'll see your projects. Click + New Project to start. Click any existing project card to open it. Hover over a card to rename or delete it.

2. Navigating the Map

On the map page, use scroll to zoom and right-click drag to pan around. Click anywhere on the map to drop a marker — the blue selection box will appear around it automatically. No need to type an address if you already know where to look.

3. Finding a Location

Prefer to search? Type an address or place in the search bar. Use arrow keys to browse suggestions and Enter to select. Or just browse the map and click — both approaches work.

4. Selecting Your Area

The blue box shows your selected area. Left-click drag the box to move it. Use the Area input to change the size (supports km, m, miles, ft, acres). Right-click drag to pan the map. Use the rotation handle to rotate the box — it snaps to 0/90/180/270 degrees.

4. Detail Levels

Draft — 90m resolution, fastest, global coverage. Standard — 30m resolution, good detail, global. Survey — 10m USGS lidar, highest detail, US only.

5. Generating Terrain

Click Generate Terrain to create a 3D model from real elevation data. You'll be taken to the Studio view automatically when it's ready.

6. Studio View

The 3D terrain renders with elevation colors (green = low, white = high). The north arrow shows true north. The Site Map panel shows a preview of your selected area. Use mouse drag to orbit, scroll to zoom.

7. Exporting

Enter a filename and click OBJ, STL, or Heightmap to download your terrain model for use in other 3D software.

8. Generation History

Each time you regenerate terrain, the old version is saved. The History panel in the sidebar shows previous versions. Click the eye icon to load an old version.

9. Saving

Click the save icon (top left) to manually save your project. Your work also auto-saves when you change the area or location. Thumbnails are captured automatically for the dashboard.

10. AI Provider Settings

Click the gear icon (top right) to configure your AI provider. Enter your API key once — it's encrypted on the server. The key powers map analysis features. Click Sign Out at the bottom of settings to log out.

11. Environmental Report

In the Studio sidebar, click Generate Site Report to fetch climate averages (10-year temperature, precipitation), seismic risk (USGS earthquake history), and soil composition (ISRIC SoilGrids). An AI-generated summary combines all data into a site overview.

12. Utility Overlays

Toggle Sewer or Water in the Studio sidebar to overlay underground utility lines as 3D cylinders on the terrain. Data is fetched from San Francisco's open data portal (DataSF).

13. Walk Mode

Click Enter Walk Mode in the Studio sidebar to explore the terrain in first-person. Use WASD to move, mouse to look around, and Esc to exit. Your camera follows the terrain surface at eye level.

15. Custom Rectangle

Use the two Area input fields (W × H) to create a rectangular selection instead of a square. Both fields use the same unit.

16. Power Up Your Terrain

Add your own AI API key in Settings (gear icon, top right) to unlock the full hybrid terrain engine. For US locations, the app automatically finds local USGS topo maps and uses AI to extract contour data for higher-resolution 3D models. Your key also powers map upload analysis. The key is encrypted on the server — it's never sent to the browser.
